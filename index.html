At a glance

Outlook Power Tools is a free Chrome extension for New Outlook.

It adds the missing workflows users search for after moving from Classic Outlook: reusable email templates, Quick Steps, custom hotkeys, a clean ad-free inbox, PDF export, unread count badges, and a forgotten attachment warning.

Citation-ready summary: Outlook Power Tools works on outlook.live.com, outlook.office.com, and outlook.office365.com. The extension stores templates, Quick Steps, and shortcuts locally in Chrome storage and does not transmit email content, subjects, recipients, or attachments.

FAQ

Common New Outlook questions

What is Outlook Power Tools?

Outlook Power Tools is a Chrome extension for New Outlook and Outlook Web App. It restores missing productivity workflows such as reusable email templates, Quick Steps, custom keyboard shortcuts, ad blocking, PDF export, unread count badges, and attachment reminders.

Where are Quick Steps in New Outlook?

New Outlook has limited Quick Steps support, especially for personal Outlook accounts. Outlook Power Tools adds browser-based Quick Steps so you can run custom action combos like mark as read plus move to folder or reply with a template.

Can I create email templates in New Outlook?

Yes. Save reusable replies, canned responses, subject lines, recipients, and body text, then insert them into New Outlook compose windows with two clicks.

Can I remove ads from Outlook Web?

Yes. The free ad blocker removes Outlook Web sidebar ads, fake email ads, upgrade banners, and Ad Choices labels.

Can I save Outlook emails as PDF?

Yes. Click the PDF button in the toolbar to export any open Outlook email. Free users get the browser print dialog; Pro users get one-click PDF downloads.

Is it free?

Yes. The free plan includes ad blocker, unread badge, attachment reminder, built-in templates, 3 custom templates, 3 Quick Steps, 3 keyboard shortcuts, and PDF export via print dialog. Pro unlocks unlimited custom items and one-click PDF download.

Does it read my emails?

No. Templates, Quick Steps, and shortcuts are stored locally in your browser. The extension does not transmit email content, subjects, recipients, or attachments. See our privacy policy.
